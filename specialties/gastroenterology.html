Overview

The Department of Gastroenterology & Endoscopy at Raj Hospitals provides top medical services for digestive system disorders including gastroenterology, hepatology, and gastrointestinal surgery. Our minimally invasive procedures like laparoscopy and endoscopy offer faster recovery, fewer complications and less post-operative follow-up. We are among few centers offering scarless minimal access surgery and advanced laparoscopic, biliary, pancreatic, and colorectal oncologic procedures.

Treatments We Offer

Comprehensive Gastroenterology care with advanced techniques and personalized treatment plans

Therapeutic and Diagnostic Upper GI Endoscopy

Advanced endoscopic procedure to examine and treat conditions of the upper digestive tract including esophagus, stomach, and duodenum.

Lower GI Endoscopy (Colonoscopy)

Comprehensive examination of the large intestine for detection and treatment of polyps, cancer, and other colonic conditions.

ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography)

Specialized procedure combining endoscopy and fluoroscopy to diagnose and treat conditions of the bile ducts and pancreas.

Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS)

Advanced imaging technique combining endoscopy with ultrasound for detailed visualization of digestive tract and surrounding organs.

Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) Clinic

Dedicated clinic for comprehensive management of Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, and other inflammatory bowel conditions.

Hepatology Clinic

Specialized care for liver diseases including hepatitis, cirrhosis, fatty liver disease, and liver failure management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Gastroenterology conditions and treatments

What symptoms indicate I should see a gastroenterologist?

Common symptoms include persistent abdominal pain, changes in bowel habits, unexplained weight loss, blood in stool, and difficulty swallowing.

How are gastroenterology procedures like endoscopy performed?

They are minimally invasive techniques performed under sedation using a flexible camera to diagnose or treat digestive system conditions.

Is laparoscopic surgery safe?

Yes, it is a safe and effective surgery with smaller incisions, faster recovery, fewer complications, and shorter hospital stays compared to open surgery.

What lifestyle changes can improve digestive health?

Eating a balanced diet, avoiding smoking and alcohol, exercising regularly, and managing stress can improve digestive function.
